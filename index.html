1. What is an NBFC?

Financial institution providing loans/insurance but not engaging in agriculture or industrial operations. Registered under Companies Act 1956; cannot accept public deposits.

2. Bank vs NBFC?

  • NBFCs cannot accept demand deposits.
  • Cannot issue cheques drawn on themselves.
  • DICGC insurance not available to NBFC depositors.

3. What is a Personal Loan?

Unsecured loan for personal expenses. No collateral required; based on creditworthiness/income. Fixed rates and monthly payments.

4. Borrowing Limit?

Depends on individual credit, income, and debt-to-income ratio. Check the specific lender portal for exact limits.

5. Documents Required

PAN, Aadhaar, Salary Slips, Permanent Address Proof, Bank Statements.

6. Early Repayment?

Yes, you can repay prior to the due date to avoid accruing additional daily interest.

7. Account Aggregator (Safe RBI System)

"Safe RBI-approved way where you allow us to check bank details online for processing."

• No money deduction
• Customer has full control
• Permission can be stopped anytime
