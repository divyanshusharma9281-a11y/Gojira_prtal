Frequently Asked Questions

Standard responses and regulatory information for internal staff.

A Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC) is a financial institution that provides various services such as loans, advances, leasing, hire-purchase, and insurance, but does not engage in activities such as agriculture, industrial operations, or the sale of goods or services.

It is also a company registered under the Companies Act 1956 and is not allowed to accept deposits from the public. Additionally, NBFCs are also known as Residuary non-banking company.

While both perform similar functions such as lending and investing, they differ in the following ways:

  • NBFCs cannot accept demand deposits.
  • NBFCs do not form part of the payment and settlement system and cannot issue cheques drawn on themselves.
  • The deposit insurance facility of Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation is not available to depositors of NBFCs, unlike in the case of banks.

A personal loan is a type of unsecured loan typically used for personal expenses (medical bills, repairs, etc.). Unlike a mortgage, personal loans do not require collateral. Lenders evaluate creditworthiness and income to determine ability to repay.

"Personal loans typically have a fixed interest rate and a fixed monthly payment, and are typically repaid over a period of one to five years."

Loan amounts and repayment periods vary based on the specific type of loan. Agents should check the latest website updates for exact figures.

Key Note:
The amount you qualify for depends on creditworthiness, income, debt-to-income ratio, and other internal lender factors.
