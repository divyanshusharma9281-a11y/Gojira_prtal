A Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC) is a financial institution that provides various banking services such as loans, credit facilities, and insurance but does not hold a full banking license. They are registered under the Companies Act and cannot accept public deposits directly.

• NBFCs cannot accept demand deposits (like savings/current accounts).

• NBFCs do not form part of the payment and settlement system.

• They cannot issue cheques drawn on themselves.
